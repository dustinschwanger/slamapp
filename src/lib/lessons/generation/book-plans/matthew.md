# Matthew Lesson Plan

## Overview

The Gospel of Matthew presents Jesus as the promised King of Israel. Written primarily for a Jewish audience, Matthew emphasizes fulfilled prophecy, Jesus' authority as Teacher and King, and the establishment of His Kingdom. Matthew contains five major teaching discourses, the most famous being the Sermon on the Mount.

## Lesson Units

Each unit covers one pericope (self-contained passage) suitable for a single 20-30 minute nursing home lesson.

### Unit 1: "The King Has Come" --- Matthew 1:18-2:12
**Theme:** God's faithfulness to His promises, Jesus as the long-awaited King, worship
**Key Truth:** God kept every promise He made about the coming Savior.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 1:18-25 (Joseph's dream and the birth)
- Matthew 2:1-12 (The wise men)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** OT prophecies fulfilled (Isaiah 7:14, Micah 5:2), significance of the gifts (gold, frankincense, myrrh), Joseph's quiet obedience

### Unit 2: "Prepare the Way" --- Matthew 3:1-17
**Theme:** Repentance, obedience, God's pleasure in His Son
**Key Truth:** God calls us to repentance, and He is well pleased with His Son.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 3:1-12 (John the Baptist's ministry)
- Matthew 3:13-17 (The baptism of Jesus)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Significance of baptism, the Trinity revealed at the Jordan, Isaiah 40:3 connection

### Unit 3: "Strength in the Word" --- Matthew 4:1-11
**Theme:** Resisting temptation through Scripture, Jesus' humanity, the power of God's Word
**Key Truth:** God's Word is our strength against every temptation.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 4:1-4 (First temptation --- stones to bread)
- Matthew 4:5-11 (Second and third temptations)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Each temptation's significance, how Jesus used Deuteronomy defensively, parallels to Israel's 40 years

### Unit 4: "Follow Me" --- Matthew 4:18-25
**Theme:** Jesus' call to discipleship, leaving everything to follow Him
**Key Truth:** Jesus calls ordinary people to follow Him, and He is worth following.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 4:18-22 (Calling of the first disciples)
- Matthew 4:23-25 (Jesus' ministry throughout Galilee)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Fishing as a trade in first-century Galilee, the significance of "immediately," the scope of Jesus' early ministry

### Unit 5: "Blessed Are You" --- Matthew 5:1-16
**Theme:** The Beatitudes --- God's upside-down kingdom values
**Key Truth:** God blesses those the world overlooks.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 5:1-9 (Beatitudes part 1)
- Matthew 5:10-16 (Persecution and being salt/light)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The setting of the Sermon on the Mount, what "blessed" means (makarios), salt and light metaphors in first-century context

### Unit 6: "Our Father in Heaven" --- Matthew 6:5-15
**Theme:** Jesus teaches how to pray
**Key Truth:** Prayer is a conversation with a loving Father, not a performance.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 6:5-8 (How not to pray)
- Matthew 6:9-15 (The Lord's Prayer)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The radical intimacy of "Father," Jewish prayer customs, why forgiveness is emphasized

### Unit 7: "Do Not Worry" --- Matthew 6:25-34
**Theme:** Trusting God with our anxieties and daily needs
**Key Truth:** The God who feeds the birds and clothes the flowers will surely care for you.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 6:25-30 (Birds of the air and lilies of the field)
- Matthew 6:31-34 (Seek first the kingdom)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Poverty and provision in first-century Palestine, what "sufficient unto the day" means practically

### Unit 8: "Come unto Me" --- Matthew 11:25-30
**Theme:** Jesus' invitation to rest for the weary
**Key Truth:** Jesus offers rest to every tired and burdened soul.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 11:25-27 (The Father and the Son)
- Matthew 11:28-30 (Come unto me, all ye that labour)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** What a "yoke" was and how it functioned, the significance of "meek and lowly in heart," rest as a present reality

### Unit 9: "The Sower and the Seed" --- Matthew 13:1-23
**Theme:** Receiving God's Word into our hearts
**Key Truth:** The condition of our hearts determines how God's Word takes root in us.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 13:1-9 (The parable)
- Matthew 13:18-23 (The explanation)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Farming in first-century Palestine, why Jesus taught in parables (vv. 10-17), the four soil types as heart conditions

### Unit 10: "Walking on Water" --- Matthew 14:22-33
**Theme:** Faith in the midst of the storm
**Key Truth:** When we keep our eyes on Jesus, He holds us up even in the storm.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 14:22-27 (Jesus walks on the water)
- Matthew 14:28-33 (Peter steps out and sinks)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The Sea of Galilee's geography and storms, the "fourth watch" timing, Peter's impulsive faith

### Unit 11: "Who Do You Say I Am?" --- Matthew 16:13-20
**Theme:** The confession of Peter --- Jesus is the Christ
**Key Truth:** The most important question anyone can answer is who Jesus is.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 16:13-16 (Peter's confession)
- Matthew 16:17-20 (You are Peter, and upon this rock)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Caesarea Philippi's pagan history, the meaning of "Christ/Messiah," what "the gates of hell shall not prevail" means

### Unit 12: "The Greatest in the Kingdom" --- Matthew 18:1-14
**Theme:** Humility, childlike faith, and God's care for the vulnerable
**Key Truth:** God treasures humility, and He will not lose a single one of His own.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 18:1-6 (Unless you become like little children)
- Matthew 18:10-14 (The parable of the lost sheep)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The status of children in the ancient world, what childlike faith looks like, the shepherd leaving the ninety-nine

### Unit 13: "Forgive Seventy Times Seven" --- Matthew 18:21-35
**Theme:** The boundless nature of forgiveness
**Key Truth:** Because God has forgiven us so much, we can forgive others.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 18:21-27 (The unmerciful servant, part 1)
- Matthew 18:28-35 (The unmerciful servant, part 2)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The size of the debt (10,000 talents = billions), Jewish teaching on forgiveness limits, the parable's warning

### Unit 14: "Hosanna to the Son of David" --- Matthew 21:1-11
**Theme:** The triumphal entry --- Jesus enters Jerusalem as King
**Key Truth:** Jesus is the King who comes in humility and peace.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 21:1-5 (Preparation and prophecy fulfilled)
- Matthew 21:6-11 (The crowd receives Jesus)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Zechariah 9:9 prophecy, significance of the donkey vs. a war horse, Palm Sunday traditions

### Unit 15: "The Two Greatest Commandments" --- Matthew 22:34-40
**Theme:** Love God, love your neighbor
**Key Truth:** All of God's law comes down to love.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 22:34-38 (Love the Lord your God)
- Matthew 22:39-40 (Love your neighbor as yourself)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The lawyer's intent, Deuteronomy 6:5 (the Shema), Leviticus 19:18, how "all the law and the prophets" hang on love

### Unit 16: "This Is My Body" --- Matthew 26:17-30
**Theme:** The Last Supper --- Jesus gives us communion
**Key Truth:** Jesus gave Himself for us, and He asks us to remember.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 26:17-25 (Preparation and the betrayal foretold)
- Matthew 26:26-30 (The bread and the cup)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Passover context and significance, old covenant/new covenant, the hymn they sang (likely Psalms 115-118)

### Unit 17: "Not My Will, but Thine" --- Matthew 26:36-46
**Theme:** Jesus in the Garden of Gethsemane
**Key Truth:** Jesus chose to obey the Father even when it cost Him everything.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 26:36-39 (Jesus prays alone)
- Matthew 26:40-46 (The disciples sleep)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** The name "Gethsemane" (oil press), the cup metaphor in OT (Isaiah 51:17), Jesus' humanity in full display

### Unit 18: "It Is Finished" --- Matthew 27:33-50
**Theme:** The crucifixion of Jesus
**Key Truth:** Jesus endured the cross because of His great love for us.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 27:33-44 (The crucifixion and mockery)
- Matthew 27:45-50 (Darkness, the cry, and death)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** Roman crucifixion practices, Psalm 22 parallels, the significance of the darkness and the torn veil (v. 51)

### Unit 19: "He Is Not Here, He Is Risen" --- Matthew 28:1-10
**Theme:** The resurrection of Jesus
**Key Truth:** Jesus is alive, and because He lives, we will live also.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 28:1-4 (The angel and the empty tomb)
- Matthew 28:5-10 (The women meet the risen Jesus)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** First-century burial customs, the significance of women as first witnesses, "Fear not" as a consistent resurrection theme

### Unit 20: "Go and Make Disciples" --- Matthew 28:16-20
**Theme:** The Great Commission
**Key Truth:** Jesus sends us to share His love and His Word with others.
**Scripture Splits:**
- Matthew 28:16-18 (The disciples worship, some doubt, all authority given)
- Matthew 28:19-20 (Go, teach, baptize, and I am with you always)
**Teacher Notes Focus:** "All authority" declaration, the trinitarian baptismal formula, "I am with you always" as Emmanuel fulfilled
